Opening its doors

in 2001, Kaya originated as a post-college dream of young Scott Gordon. Kaya is a neighborhood coffee and tea shop located close to the CMU campus and Mt. Pleasant community. Serving fair trade coffee from Equal Exchange and healthy fresh food, Kaya soon took on a life of its own.

Its cozy, comfortable atmosphere gave students, professors, and neighbors a place to work, study, caffeniate, eat, relax, and make friends. In 2008, Scott passed the keys on to the Cotton family who currently, along with the dedicated baristas and customers, keep the doors open.

Kaya believes in doing as little harm as possible. So we are dedicated to serving fairly traded coffee and tea as often as possible, using beans from Equal Exchange in all our espresso drinks. Fairly traded means we may pay a little more for our coffee and tea to help insure that the farmers growing it will be paid fairly. Our loose leaf teas all come from Arbor Teas, a Michigan-based tea company also focusing on organic, fair trade tea. Our food is purchased from local vendors whenever possible, or made right here at Kaya.

Locally, we participate in the Mt. Pleasant Recycling Program and applaud the city’s curbside pick up. Our milk jugs, cans, bottles, plastic, cardboard and paper all go to the Recycling Facility thanks to our city’s efforts to keep Mt. Pleasant even more pleasant. Local gardeners recycle our coffee grounds.

Supporting the local arts, we host Open Mic nights on Wednesday night during college sessions, Old Time Music jams, Irish music jams, and a Mid-Month Music Series featuring area musicians. Check the event calendar to see when these are happening.

On our “Featured Artist” board, we have a rotating art display from CMU students, professors, and area artists. We also host Poetry Slams and musical fund-raisers dedicated to generating funds for such worthy goals as the Isabella County Food Pantry and the Haiti Project Against Poverty.

One of our favorite regulars, 99-year-young Brian Wood discovered Kaya about 5 years ago on a neighborhood walk to ease his arthritis. He said, “I had to go on a walk. I saw this place, so I came in. People were nice to me, so I’ve been coming back.” We have been honored with CM Life’s “Best Coffee Shop” Award for the past several years, but we think Brian says it best.

We hope you walk in, and then keep on coming back.
